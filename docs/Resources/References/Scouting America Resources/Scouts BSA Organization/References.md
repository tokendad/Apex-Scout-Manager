# Scouts BSA Troop Positions

## Youth Positions of Responsibility
These leadership roles are held by the Scouts themselves. Service in these positions is often a requirement for rank advancement (Star, Life, and Eagle).

### Senior Patrol Leader (SPL)
The top youth leader in the troop. They are elected by the Scouts to represent them and are responsible for running all troop meetings, events, activities, and the annual program planning conference. The SPL also runs the Patrol Leaders' Council (PLC) meetings.

### Assistant Senior Patrol Leader (ASPL)
The second-highest-ranking junior leader. The ASPL acts as the Senior Patrol Leader in their absence and provides leadership to other junior leaders. They help train and supervise roles such as the Scribe, Quartermaster, Instructor, Librarian, Historian, and Chaplain Aide.

### Patrol Leader (PL)
The elected leader of a specific patrol. They represent their patrol at all Patrol Leaders' Council meetings and the annual program planning conference. They are responsible for planning and leading patrol meetings and activities and keeping their members informed.

### Troop Guide
A mentor and leader for the **New-Scout Patrol**. They introduce new Scouts to troop operations, help them earn the First Class rank, and coach the new-Scout patrol leader on their duties.

### Quartermaster
The supply boss. They are responsible for keeping track of troop equipment and ensuring it is in good working order. They issue equipment for outings, ensure it is returned, and make suggestions for new or replacement items.

### Scribe
The troop secretary. They keep the troop records, including the minutes of the Patrol Leaders' Council (PLC) meetings. They also track dues, advancement records, and Scout attendance at troop meetings and activities.

### Den Chief
An older Scout who works with a Cub Scout den (younger children in a separate Pack). They assist the Den Leaders with activities, serve as a role model, and encourage Cub Scouts to bridge over to the Scouts BSA troop upon graduation.

### Chaplain Aide
Works with the Troop Chaplain to meet the religious needs of Scouts. They help plan religious observances for campouts and courts of honor and promote the religious emblems program.

### Historian
The troop archivist. They keep a historical record or scrapbook of troop activities, gather photos and facts, and take care of troop trophies and souvenirs.

### Instructor
An older Scout proficient in specific skills (e.g., First Aid, Knots, Camping). They teach these Scouting skills to other Scouts to help them advance in rank.

### Librarian
Manages the troop's literature. They set up and maintain the troop library (merit badge books, pamphlets), keep records of what is owned, and manage the check-in/check-out system.

### Bugler
Sounds bugle calls as required (e.g., Taps, Reveille) at troop meetings and campouts.

### Order of the Arrow (OA) Representative
Serves as a communication link between the troop and the local Order of the Arrow lodge (Scouting's national honor society).

### Outdoor Ethics Guide
Helps the troop plan and conduct outdoor programs that emphasize effectively practicing the principles of *Leave No Trace* and the *Outdoor Code*.

### Junior Assistant Scoutmaster (JASM)
A Scout at least 16 years old who supervises and supports other boy leaders. They perform duties similar to an Assistant Scoutmaster but are still youth members.

## Adult Positions
While the troop is "youth-led," adults provide safety, mentorship, and logistical support.

### Scoutmaster
The adult leader responsible for the image and program of the troop. They train and guide the youth leaders (especially the SPL) and ensure the rules of the BSA are followed.

### Assistant Scoutmaster
Adults assigned specific duties by the Scoutmaster to help the troop run smoothly. They may oversee specific programs or patrols.

### Committee Chair
The top adult administrative officer. They recruit other adults to the committee and run the monthly committee meetings to handle the business side of the troop (finances, logistics, policies).

### Committee Members
Adult volunteers who handle specific administrative tasks such as Treasurer (finances), Advancement (tracking rank progress), Equipment, or Membership.

### Chartered Organization Representative (COR)
The liaison between the troop and the organization that sponsors it (e.g., a church, school, or civic group). They ensure the troop operates in accordance with the chartered organization's values and the BSA's standards.
